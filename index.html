Day 1 — Saturday

Zurich → Milan

🏨 Accommodation: Milan
10:30 Arrival

Zurich Airport

"One of Europe's slickest airports—you'll actually enjoy it."

Land at 10:30 and clear one of Europe's most efficient airports. Expect to be through immigration and at the baggage carousel within 40 minutes. Zurich Airport is immaculate, well-signed, and stress-free—a perfect entry to Switzerland.

11:20 – 15:00 City Walk

Zurich City Layover

"Swiss precision meets lakeside beauty—a city that earns every cliché."

Hop the direct train to Zurich HB in 10 minutes. Stroll the Bahnhofstrasse—Europe's most expensive shopping street—then walk the Limmat River into the Altstadt (Old Town). End at Lake Zurich for a coffee with a view before heading back.

💡 Local Tip The Zurich Card (24h) covers all transit from the airport plus free museum entry. Worth it for a half-day stop.
15:30 Transfer

Train to Milan (SBB/Trenitalia)

"Crossing the Alps by rail is a travel highlight in itself."

The 15:30 direct IC threads through the Gotthard Base Tunnel—the world's longest railway tunnel—and descends into Italy's Po Valley. Arrive ~19:05, drop bags, and catch the Duomo illuminated at night.

Day 2 — Sunday

Milan

🏨 Accommodation: Milan
09:30 Must-See Art

The Last Supper

"15 minutes with Leonardo—and every second will stay with you forever."

Leonardo da Vinci's Il Cenacolo is housed in the refectory of Santa Maria delle Grazie. Visits are tightly controlled: maximum 30 people at a time, 15 minutes inside. The experience is hushed, nearly sacred.

⚠️ Book ahead vivaticket.com. Tickets sell out 2–3 months in advance for peak season.
11:30 History

Sforza Castle

"A fortress that shaped the Renaissance—and holds a Michelangelo most people miss."

Built in the 15th century, this massive red-brick castle houses Michelangelo's unfinished Pietà Rondanini, his final sculpture before his death.

14:30 Icon

Duomo & Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II

"Walk the rooftop spires at sunset—Milan's skyline belongs to you."

The Duomo bristles with 3,400 statues and 135 spires. Take the elevator to the rooftop to wander among the marble pinnacles. Afterwards, duck into the Galleria—Italy's oldest active shopping mall.

Day 3 — Monday

Milan → Venice

🏨 Accommodation: Venice
08:30 Transfer

High-Speed Train to Venice

"The train pulls into a lagoon. No roads. Pure magic from the first second."

The final approach is unforgettable—the rail bridge crosses open water for 4km, and you step off the train directly onto the banks of the Grand Canal.

11:30 – 17:00 Explore

St. Mark's, Doge's Palace & Rialto

"Get lost on purpose—the best Venice moments happen when you stop following a map."

Visit the Byzantine mosaic interior of the Basilica. Walk the Bridge of Sighs in the Doge's Palace. Dive into the warren of sestieri; each turn reveals a new canal.

💡 Avoid the crowds St. Mark's is mobbed 10am–3pm. Go before 9am or after 5pm for a quieter experience.
18:00 Golden Hour

Sunset on the Lagoon

"Venice turns to copper at dusk. Find a quiet fondamenta and just sit."

Walk to the Zattere promenade for the best sunset views. Dinner in Dorsoduro, away from the tourist crush: try sarde in saor.

Day 4 — Tuesday

Venice Islands Day Trip

🏨 Accommodation: Venice
09:00 Island Escape

Burano

"The most photogenic place in Italy—and the pasta is even better than the views."

Burano is a fishing island where every house is painted a different bold colour. The tradition supposedly helped fishermen spot their homes through fog.

13:30 Ancient History

Torcello Island

"The island that was Venice before Venice existed—hauntingly, beautifully empty."

Torcello contains an extraordinary 7th-century cathedral with some of the finest Byzantine mosaics in Europe.

17:30 Evening

Venice by Night

"When the day-trippers leave, Venice becomes yours."

As evening falls, the tour groups thin and reflections on the canals come alive. Wind through Cannaregio for a quiet bacaro.

Day 5 — Wednesday

Venice → Florence

🏨 Accommodation: Florence
08:00 Transfer

Train to Florence

"Two hours, and you travel from watery Gothic to golden Renaissance."

Florence is compact and walkable—most major sites are within 15 minutes of each other.

11:00 Architecture

Duomo Climb & Ponte Vecchio

"Climbing inside Brunelleschi's dome is like walking through engineering genius."

The 463-step climb winds between the inner and outer shell of the dome. Then walk to the Ponte Vecchio—the only Florentine bridge not destroyed in 1944.

15:00 World-Class Art

Uffizi Gallery

"Room 10: Botticelli's Birth of Venus will actually stop you in your tracks."

Arguably the world's greatest repository of Renaissance painting. Botticelli, Leonardo, Caravaggio—plan 2–3 hours minimum.

Day 6 — Thursday

Tuscany Day Trip

🏨 Accommodation: Florence
09:00 Medieval City

Siena

"Better preserved than Rome, less crowded than Florence—Siena is Tuscany's best-kept secret."

The heart of Siena is the shell-shaped Piazza del Campo. Don't leave without tasting ricciarelli biscuits.

14:30 Medieval Towers

San Gimignano

"Manhattan, 800 years earlier—a skyline of stone towers over rolling Tuscan hills."

Famous for its 14 surviving medieval towers. Wealthy families built them as symbols of power—an early arms race in stone.

18:30 Evening Return

Back to Florence for Dinner

"Bistecca alla Fiorentina: a 1kg T-bone cooked over wood fire, medium rare, no negotiation."

The iconic steak is always from Chianina cattle and always rare. Pair with a glass of Brunello di Montalcino.

Day 7 — Friday

Florence → Rome

🏨 Accommodation: Rome
08:30 Transfer

Train to Rome

"90 minutes from the Renaissance to the Roman Empire."

Rome's layers are unique: a papal baroque city wrapped around a medieval city built on top of an ancient one.

11:30 Ancient Rome

Colosseum & Roman Forum

"Stand where 50,000 Romans cheered. History isn't abstract here—it's under your feet."

The Colosseum (72 AD) held up to 80,000 spectators for gladiatorial contests. Walk along the Sacred Way past temples in the Forum.

15:30 Roman Icons

Pantheon & Trevi Fountain

"The Pantheon's dome is still the world's greatest unreinforced concrete structure—built in 125 AD."

The oculus at the top of the Pantheon is the only source of light. Then walk to the Trevi Fountain—throw one coin to return to Rome.

Day 8 — Saturday

Vatican City

🏨 Accommodation: Rome
09:00 Sacred Icon

St. Peter's Basilica & Dome

"The largest church on Earth—and the climb to the dome gives Rome to you in one sweep."

The Basilica holds Michelangelo's Pietà. Entry is free—but arrive by 9am before the queues form.

11:30 Michelangelo

Sistine Chapel & Vatican Museums

"Michelangelo painted the ceiling lying on his back. Spend 4 years and see what he made."

The Museums feature the Gallery of Maps and finally—the Sistine Chapel with its famous ceiling and Last Judgment altar wall.

16:00 Evening Rome

Piazza Navona & Trastevere

"Trastevere at night: fairy lights, cobblestones, laughter—this is the real Rome."

Cross the Tiber to Trastevere for dinner. Eat cacio e pepe: just pecorino, black pepper, and pasta water.

Day 9 — Sunday

Rome → Zurich | Departure

✈️ Flight home
Morning Final Hours

Last Walk Through Rome

"Rome rewards the slow walker. The best thing about leaving is knowing you'll come back."

Grab a cornetto with a standing espresso at a local bar. Pick up souvenirs at Campo de' Fiori market.

🚌 Airport transfer Leonardo Express train from Roma Termini to FCO takes 32 minutes. Allow 3h before departure.
Departure Homeward

Flight Rome (FCO) → Zurich

"The Alps from above are the last postcard. Keep your eyes on the window."

Arrive in Zurich with 9 days of extraordinary memories—and a mental list of everything you'll do differently next time.
